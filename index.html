How it works

Curve Swap in three steps

Curve Swap keeps the flow direct: connect a wallet, pick the asset pair, review the live quote and sign only when the trade looks right.

Step 01

Connect your wallet

Open the app with an Ethereum wallet and stay in control of approvals, signatures and funds from your own address.

Step 02

Choose the pool route

Select the stablecoin or pegged asset you want to trade. The app uses available pool liquidity from the stable-swap protocol.

Step 03

Review and confirm

Check the quote, expected output, slippage setting and network cost in the app, then approve and confirm the swap from your wallet.

Why Curve Swap

Got questions?

Curve Swap FAQ

The essentials on stablecoin swaps, supported assets, timing, cost and safety before you connect a wallet.

Open App
01

What is Curve Swap?

Curve Swap is a non-custodial DeFi swap app for trading stablecoins and similarly priced crypto assets through liquidity pools while keeping wallet control.

02

Which assets and chains can I use?

Supported assets depend on available pools and networks, including stablecoins, wrapped assets and chain-specific deployments. See the live list on the supported chains page.

03

How long does a swap take?

Most timing depends on wallet confirmation, network congestion and transaction finality. The app shows the live transaction state after you submit a swap.

04

What does Curve Swap cost?

The app displays the live quote, slippage impact and network cost before you sign. External data such as DeFi liquidity dashboards can help you understand broader pool conditions, but the app quote is what matters for your transaction.

05

How do I use Curve Swap safely?

Verify the asset, chain, pool route and approval request before signing. Keep wallet software current, use a trusted connection and avoid signing transactions you do not understand.
